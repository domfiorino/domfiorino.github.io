Internet Connectivity Issues

Follow the collapsibles below to find a solution

Slow Internet

Slow internet access in certain rooms

- A router broadcasts in all directions from a central location, so moving the router to a more central location in the house may improve overall connectivity within the house. Position the router higher, farther away from other devices.
- Adjust external antennas on the router, if any, to point toward the desired location/room.
- Use a WiFi analyzer to determine if your network is overlapping with someone.
- Purchasing a WiFi range extender to extend the range of main router; purchase a new one.

Slow internet everywhere

Log onto a computer that is connected to the modem or plug in laptop directly to the modem and run an internet speed test.
- If the speeds are still slow, then the problem is probably with your internet connection, not the router; contact your internet service provider.
- If the speeds are much better, then try the following:

1) The WiFi network may be overcrowded by devices or by nearby networks.
- Disconnect unused devices from WiFi.
- If you have a Dual Band router, enable both 2.4GHz and 5.0 GHz. Connect compatible devices to the 5.0 GHz network in order to decrease interference on both networks.
- Change the router channel.
- Connect devices via ethernet cable to the router to free up WiFi.

2) You may need to perform a factory reset on router by pressing and holding the reset button on the router for 30 seconds. Wait until the status lights are lit back up.

3) The router may be dying and it is time for a new one.

No Internet

Certain device cannot connect

This is probably just momentary. Try the following:

- Turn the WiFi on the device off, then back on.

- Unplug the power cord from the router for 30 seconds, then plug it back in.

- Delete the network from the list of saved networks and try to connect again.

- Follow OS (operating system) specific instructions.
- If you’re running Windows 10, search for “WiFi Troubleshooting” and open the result, which should be Identify and Repair Network Issues. That will go through a series of diagnostics that may restore connectivity.
- On MacOS, you can run Wireless Diagnostics. Hold the Options key and click the AirPort (Wi-Fi) icon on the menu bar. Find Open Wireless Diagnostics, and then follow the on-screen instructions.

- Reboot the device.

- Check for viruses, which may be slowing down your computer.

Nothing can connect

- Make sure the router is fully connected and is on (green status light is on).
- Then, plug in the device directly into the router via ethernet cable.

If this fixes the issue, then the problem is with the WiFi. It may be time to get a new router.

If the problem persists after connecting to the router, then the internet may be down altogether. These are the next steps:
- Check your Internet Service Provider's webpage, social accounts, or call them to see if their services are temporarily down.
- Alternatively, use a service monitoring site such as Downdetector to see the status of a specific network.

If the problem still persists:
- Unplug the power cord to router for 30 seconds, then plug it back in. Wait a minute for it to boot back up. Try to connect to the internet.
- Perform a factory reset on the router by pressing and holding the reset button for 30 seconds. Try to connect.
- It may be time to get a new router.

Connection Drops/Crashes

Connection drops at random times

- There could be interference from other networks or devices, including a microwave if the network operates on a 2.4 GHz band; consider changing the WiFi router channel.
- Unplug the power cord to the router for 30 seconds, then plug it back in. Test the connection.
- Factory reset the router by pressing and holding the reset button for 30 seconds.

Router Crashes Regularly

- Factory reset the router by pressing and holding the reset button for 30 seconds.
- It may be time for a new router.

WiFi drops when logging back into computer

- If you are running Windows, turn off Fast Startup.

Other

Forgot WiFi password

- You will have to factory reset the router by pressing and holding the reset button for 30 seconds; any custom WiFi settings will be lost.

Unknown Devices on Network

If you believe that there are unknown users on your network, follow the steps below:

1) Log into your router's WiFi app or administrator settings on the computer.
2) Look for unknown devices on the network.
3) Block these devices by name or by MAC address if necessary.
4) Change the WiFi password.
5) Save the changes then reboot the router either by selecting a "Reboot" option or unplugging the power cord from the router for 30 seconds and then plugging it back in.
