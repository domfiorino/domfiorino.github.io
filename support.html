Internet Connectivity Issues

Follow the collapsibles below to find a solution

Slow Internet

Slow internet access in certain rooms

1) A router broadcasts in all directions from a central location, so moving the router to a more central location in the house may improve overall connectivity within the house

Position router higher, farther away from other devices
2) Adjust external antennas if there are any
3) Use a WiFi analyzer to determine if your network is overlapping with someone
4) Purchasing a wifi repeater to extend the range of main router; purchase a new one

Slow internet everywhere

Log onto computer that is connected to modem or plug in laptop directly to modem and run a Speedtest by Ookla
- If the speeds are still slow, then the problem is probably with your internet connection, not the router; contact ISP
- If the speeds are much better, then try the following steps:

1) The WiFi network may be overcrowded by devices or by nearby networks
- Change the router channel
- Disconnect unused devices from WiFi
- Connect devices via ethernet to free up WiFi
- If a Dual Band router, enable both 2.4GHz and 5.0 GHz channels

2) May need to perform a factory reset on router

3) Router may be dying and it is time for a new one

No Internet

Certain device cannot connect

This is probably just momentary. Try:

1) Turning WiFi on device off, then back on

2) If doesnt work then unplug router for 30 secs then plug back in

3) Deleting network from list of saved networks and try to connect again

4) Follow OS specific instructions
- If you’re running Windows 10, search for “wifi troubleshooting” and open the result, which should be Identify and Repair Network Issues. That will go through a series of diagnostics that may restore connectivity.
- On MacOS, you can run Wireless Diagnostics. Hold the Options key and click the AirPort (Wi-Fi) icon on the menu bar. Find Open Wireless Diagnostics, and then follow the on-screen instructions.

5) Reboot the device

6) Check for viruses, which may be slowing down your computer

Nothing can connect

1) Make sure router is fully connected and is on
2) Plug in device directly to router via ethernet cable

If this fixes the issue, then the problem is with the WiFi

If the problem persists after connecting to the router, then the internet may be down altogether. These are the next steps.
Check your Internet Service Provider's webpage, social accounts, or call them to see if their services are temporarily down
Alternatively, use a monitoring site such as Downdetector to see the status of a specific network

If the problem still persists:
Unplug the router for 30 seconds, then plug it back in. Wait a minute for it to boot back up. Try to connect to the internet.
Perform a factory reset on the router. Try to connect
It may be time to get a new router.

Connection Drops/Crashes

Connection drops at random times

1) There could possibly be interference from other networks or devices, including a microwave if the network operates on a 2.4 GHz band; consider changing the WiFi router channel (use NetSpot or Wifi Analyzer).
2) Unplug the power cord to the router for 30 seconds, then plug it back in. Test the connection.
3) Factory reset the router

Router Crashes Regularly

1) Factory reset the router
2) May need a new router

WiFi drops when logging back into computer

1) If you are running Windows, turn off fast start-up

Other

Forgot WiFi password

1) You will have to factory reset the router

Unknown Devices on Network

If you believe that there are unknown users on your network, follow these steps below:

1) Log into wifi app or administrator settings, which can be found with IP address
2) Look for unknown devices
3) Block the MAC addresses of these devices
4) Change wifi password
5) Reboot router
